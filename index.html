Florence Italy

From the Renaissance to the heart of Tuscany, Florence blends world-class art, timeless architecture, a long history, and a lively food scene across the Arno River

History

Originally founded as“Florentina” by the Romans, Florence rose to wealth from its prominence among banking families like the Medici. From the 14th to the 16th centuries, its workshops and studios produced talent from top-tier artists like Brunelleschi, Donatello, and Michelangelo. This established the city as a beacon of art.

Culture

Florence is very compact, yet it features many layers of the city. The Duomo features grand architecture, the Oltrarno is known for its artisan cafes, Santa Croce and San Lorenzo leather markets. Some year-round festivals and museums keep the city strong all the time.

Landmarks

  2. Uffizi Gallery
  3. Ponte Vecchio
